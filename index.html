GLP-1 & Metabolic Peptides

Tirzepatide vs. Semaglutide — head-to-head weight loss

Strong evidence

A 2025 systematic review and meta-analysis (7 studies, ~29,000 participants) found tirzepatide outperformed semaglutide for weight reduction, with a clear dose-response relationship. Backed by both RCTs and large real-world cohorts.

Semaglutide for weight loss without diabetes

Strong evidence

Systematic review and meta-analysis of RCTs in overweight/obese adults without diabetes confirms consistent, clinically meaningful weight loss versus placebo — this is the best-studied peptide category on this page.

Peptides Still Being Studied

BPC-157 — where the human evidence actually stands

Limited human data

Despite decades of promising animal research on healing and tissue repair, only a handful of small human pilot studies exist as of 2026. A 2025 systematic review found 35 preclinical studies to just 1 clinical study. Not FDA-approved for any use.

Read the 2026 update →

CJC-1295 + Ipamorelin — the growth hormone stack

Limited human data

Two small human trials (2006, 2013) show low weekly doses raise GH secretion, but no large RCT has tested the combination for body composition outcomes. Both remain investigational, not FDA-approved.

Read the clinical overview →

Thymosin alpha-1 — the most clinically documented peptide here

Emerging

Approved for clinical use in 35+ countries (not the U.S.) and studied in RCTs for hepatitis, sepsis, and immune support. A 2025 meta-analysis found it reduced 28-day mortality in sepsis patients — genuinely one of the stronger evidence bases in the peptide space.

Read the meta-analysis →

NAD+ — does it actually do what it claims for aging?

Mixed evidence

A 2024 RCT showed increased blood NAD+ levels and improved quality-of-life scores after 28 days. But a separate double-blind cognition trial found no benefit over placebo, and metabolic/functional outcomes remain inconsistent across trials.

FDA-Approved Peptide Medications

PT-141 (bremelanotide) for HSDD

Strong evidence (women)

FDA-approved for premenopausal women with hypoactive sexual desire disorder — trials showed consistent improvement in desire and distress, though it didn't meaningfully increase satisfying sexual events. Use in men remains off-label with more limited trial data.

Supplements & Labs

Creatine may benefit women's cognition more than men's

Strong evidence

A 2024 meta-analysis of 16 RCTs found creatine improved memory, attention, and processing speed — with subgroup analysis showing greater benefit in women, whose baseline creatine stores run lower to begin with.

Read the meta-analysis →

The 2024 Endocrine Society vitamin D guideline

Mixed / debated

Updated 2024 guidance recommends empiric supplementation only for specific groups (ages 1–18, 75+, pregnancy, prediabetes) rather than universal screening — and the guideline itself has drawn public rebuttal from other researchers. Good example of how "evidence-based" still involves real debate.

Read the guideline →

Magnesium glycinate for sleep and anxiety

Emerging

Growing RCT evidence (2024–2025) shows modest sleep quality improvements and reduced perceived anxiety, particularly in people with lower baseline magnesium. Effects are real but modest — not a sedative.
